First Time Gardening

On this page, I will walk you through my experience gardening for the first time. I will discuss the times or seasons I started gardening, where I planted my plants, the tools and materials I used, and why I decided to start gardening.

Season

The best time to start gardening in the U.S. is in spring, which is what I did. This is when the soil begins to warm up and the days start getting longer. Additionally, many vegetables, herbs, and flowers thrive when planted after the last frost.

Location

Choosing the right location to start my garden was one of the most important steps for its success. I decided to plant my garden in my backyard because it receives 6 to 8 hours of direct sunlight, has good access to water making watering easy, is protected from harsh winds, and is easily accessible for me to tend to my plants.

How Did I Start

There are many gardening styles to choose from, depending on your space, budget, and goals. Some popular styles include traditional in-ground gardening, raised bed gardening, container gardening, vertical gardening, and hydroponic gardening. I chose to create a raised bed garden using the square foot method because it is beginner-friendly, easy to access, and allows for better control over weeds and pests. To start my garden, I used a variety of tools and materials, including gardening gloves, a measuring tape, a pencil, a shovel, a saw, a drill, lumber, soil, compost, and string.
