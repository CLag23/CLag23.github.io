First Time Gardening

On this page, I will walk you through my experience gardening for the first time. I will discuss the times or seasons I started gardening, where I planted my plants, the tools and materials I used, and why I decided to start gardening.

Season

The best time to start gardening in the U.S. is usually in spring, which is when I got started. As the weather warms up and the days get longer, the soil starts to thaw out and becomes perfect for planting. A lot of veggies like lettuce, peas, and spinach love the cooler spring temps, while others like tomatoes, peppers, and squash do best once it gets a bit warmer. Location

Choosing the right location to start my garden was one of the most important steps for its success. I decided to plant my garden in my backyard because it receives 6 to 8 hours of direct sunlight, has good access to water making watering easy, is protected from harsh winds, and is easily accessible for me to tend to my plants.

How Did I Start

There are many gardening styles to choose from, depending on your space, budget, and goals. Some popular styles include traditional in-ground gardening, raised bed gardening, container gardening, vertical gardening, and hydroponic gardening. I chose to create a raised bed garden using the square foot method because it is beginner-friendly, easy to access, and allows for better control over weeds and pests. To start my garden, I used a variety of tools and materials, including gardening gloves, a measuring tape, a pencil, a shovel, a saw, a drill, lumber, Top soil, compost, and string.

Why

I decided to start gardening for several reasons. First, I wanted to learn how to grow my own fresh and healthy produce, which is often more nutritious and flavorful than store-bought options. Second, I was inspired by a relative who had years of experience in gardening and generously shared their knowledge and passion with me. Finally, I wanted to find something new to do during the summer, and gardening seemed like an enjoyable activity that doesn‚Äôt require too much maintenance if set up correctly.
